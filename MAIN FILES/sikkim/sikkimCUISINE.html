CUISINES


Sikkim offers a unique blend of traditional Sikkimese, Tibetan, Nepalese, and Bhutanese cuisines. Popular dishes-

a)Momos:-

Steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat, served with spicy chutney.

b)Thukpa :-

A hearty noodle soup with vegetables or meat, perfect for the cold climate

c)Sinki :-

A fermented radish soup, often enjoyed as a comforting side dish.

d)Phagshapa:-

A pork-based dish cooked with radishes and dried chilies, known for its tangy and smoky flavor. 4 Gundruk A fermented leafy vegetable curry, rich in probiotics and unique in taste.

e)Sel Roti:-

A traditional sweet rice flour bread, deep-fried and crispy. 7 Sha Phaley, Deep-fried bread stuffed with minced meat or cabbage, crispy on the outside and soft inside.

f)Kinema Curry:-

A fermented soybean curry, packed with protein and earthy flavors. 9 Chhurpi Soup, A soup made from local fermented cheese, offering a tangy and creamy taste.

g)Sael Roti with Aloo Dum:-

Crispy rice bread paired with spiced potato curry, a favorite breakfast dish.

h)Niguru with Chhurpi:-

A dish made with fiddlehead ferns and local cheese, combining earthy and tangy flavors. 12 Chang (Thomba) A traditional millet-based alcoholic beverage, served warm.

i)Dhindo:-

A thick porridge made from buckwheat or millet flour, often eaten with curries or pickles.

j)Bamboo Shoot Curry:-

A curry made with fresh bamboo shoots, offering a distinctive tangy taste. 13 Chhurpi-Ningro Curry, A delightful combination of local cheese and wild ferns, full of unique textures.
