ART & CRAFTS


The local art and craft of Sikkim are deeply influenced by its Tibetan Buddhist traditions and the natural surroundings.

a)THANGKA PAINTINGS: :-

Thangka is a traditional Tibetan Buddhist painting on fabric, often depicting deities, mandalas, or religious scenes. These are highly detailed and require precision and patience to create. They are considered sacred and are used in religious ceremonies.

b) MASKS AND STATUETTES:-

Sikkimese artisans create colorful wooden masks for various traditional festivals and dances. These masks often represent gods, demons, or mythological figures, and are used in the Cham Dance, which is a Buddhist ritual dance.

c) HANDWOVEN TEXTILES:-

Sikkim has a rich tradition of handloom weaving, and textiles made from wool and cotton are commonly used to create shawls, carpets, and jackets. The Chhegu fabric, woven by the locals, is often used for traditional attire, such as Phulukari (women’s dresses) and Tungbuk (men’s jackets).

d) METALWORK:-

Traditional metalcraft, including casting and engraving, is widely practiced in Sikkim. Local artisans craft brass and copper items, such as prayer wheels, incense holders, and religious symbols.

e) WOOD CARVING:-

Wood carving is a traditional craft in Sikkim, and artisans carve intricate patterns into wood for making furniture, doors, windows, and religious artifacts. The craftsmanship often features motifs such as dragons, lotus flowers, and deities.

f) PAPER CRAFT:-

Dokra is a traditional Sikkimese paper craft made from handmade paper, which is often used to create decorations and various artifacts.

g) TIBETAN RUGS AND CARPETS :-

The weaving of woolen carpets and rugs, often made in bright colors with intricate patterns, is a well-known craft in Sikkim. These carpets are used both for decorative purposes and in daily life.
