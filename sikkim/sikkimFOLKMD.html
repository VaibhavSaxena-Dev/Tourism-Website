FOLK MUSIC


a)Lepcha Music :-

The Lepcha community, one of the indigenous tribes of Sikkim, has traditional music that includes the use of indigenous instruments like the Tongba (a flute) and Damphu (a drum). The music is often related to spiritual rituals and daily life.

b) Bhutia Music :-

The Bhutia people, who are of Tibetan origin, have music that reflects their Buddhist culture. Their folk songs are often spiritual and are performed using instruments like the Yangchen (Tibetan lute), Daman (drum), and Tungna (a stringed instrument). These songs are sometimes accompanied by prayers and rituals.

c) Nepali Music:-

As many Nepali-speaking people reside in Sikkim, Nepali folk music is also quite popular. Dohori is a traditional form of Nepali folk music where two groups sing songs to each other, often accompanied by instruments like Sarangi (a string instrument) and Madal (a percussion instrument).

FOLK DANCE


a) Mask Dance (Cham Dance):

A traditional dance performed by Buddhist monks, usually during religious festivals. Dancers wear elaborate masks and costumes to portray deities and mythological figures. It is performed to the rhythmic beats of drums and cymbals.

b)Dumang Dance:

A celebratory folk dance of the Lepcha community performed during festivals and special occasions. It is a group dance where people form a circle, holding hands, and move in harmony to the rhythm of drums and flutes.

c)Singhi Chham Dance:

A popular dance of the Bhutia community, performed during the Losar (Tibetan New Year) and other festivals. The dancers wear masks representing lions and perform in a very energetic and vigorous manner. The dance is meant to ward off evil spirits.

d)Chhewar Dance :

Performed by the youth of the Nepali community during the Chhewar ceremony, a rite of passage for boys. It involves lively, rhythmic movements, often in pairs or groups, and is accompanied by traditional Nepali folk music.

e)Maruni Dance:

A traditional Nepali dance performed by women, often during festivals like Dashain and Tihar. Dancers wear colorful attire and move in graceful steps to the beats of drums and cymbals.
